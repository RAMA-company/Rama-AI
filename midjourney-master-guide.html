Midjourney Master Guide: Beginner to Pro

By Rama Empire • September 16, 2025

Midjourney has emerged as one of the most powerful and accessible tools for generating stunning images from text. What was once a niche skill reserved for digital artists is now a creative superpower available to everyone. While getting started is as simple as a single command, unlocking the full potential of Midjourney requires an understanding of its underlying principles. This guide will walk you through the journey from a complete beginner, creating your first image, to a seasoned professional, crafting breathtaking masterpieces with precision and control.

The Basics: Your First Prompt

A prompt in Midjourney is the text you provide to describe the image you want to create. Think of it as a conversation with an incredibly talented but literal artist. The more descriptive you are, the better the result will be.

1. Start with the Core Subject

Begin by describing the main subject of your image. This is the most important part of your prompt and sets the stage for everything else.

  • Example: "A majestic dragon"

2. Add Style and Context

Next, add descriptive words to give the AI more to work with. Think about the style, the setting, the lighting, and the mood.

  • Example: "A majestic dragon, in the style of fantasy art, flying over a volcano at sunset."

Advanced Parameters: Fine-Tuning Your Vision

Parameters are commands you add to the end of your prompt to control specific aspects of the image generation process. They are powerful tools for fine-tuning your results.

  • Aspect Ratio (--ar): Use this to control the width and height of the image. --ar 16:9 creates a wide cinematic image, while --ar 9:16 is perfect for a mobile-friendly vertical shot.
  • Stylize (--s): This parameter controls how much artistic flair Midjourney adds to your image. A lower value (--s 50) produces a more literal result, while a higher value (--s 750) gives the AI more creative freedom.
  • Chaos (--c): Use this to control the level of variation in the initial image grid. A low value (--c 0) gives you very similar images, while a high value (--c 100) results in wild, unpredictable variations.

Next-Level Techniques: Going Pro

Once you're comfortable with basic prompts and parameters, you can start experimenting with more advanced techniques to create truly unique and complex images.

Multi-Prompting

This technique allows you to specify the relative importance of different elements in your prompt. By using double colons (::), you can separate ideas and assign them a weight.

  • Example: "A futuristic cityscape::5 with a serene cherry blossom tree::1"

In this example, the AI will prioritize the "futuristic cityscape" five times more than the "serene cherry blossom tree."

Image Prompts and Blending

Midjourney can use images as part of its prompt. By uploading an image and including it in your prompt, you can use its style, composition, or subject matter to influence your final result. You can also use the /blend command to combine two to five images into a single new image.

  • Example: "[Image URL] A photorealistic portrait, intricate details"

Conclusion: Your Skill, Your Power

Midjourney is a canvas for your imagination, and the prompt is your brush. By mastering these foundational and advanced techniques, you can move beyond simple generations and begin to truly shape the AI's output to match your artistic vision. The key is to experiment, iterate, and refine. Start with a simple idea, add layers of detail and control with parameters, and don't be afraid to try new combinations. With this guide, you have the knowledge to create anything you can imagine. Now, it's time to create.
